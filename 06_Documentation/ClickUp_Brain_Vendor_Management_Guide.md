## üìä Vendor Categories and Classification

### Vendor Categories

**Strategic Vendors**:
- **Definition**: Critical to business operations and strategy
- **Characteristics**: High spend, high impact, long-term relationship
- **Examples**: Cloud providers, core software vendors, key service providers
- **Management Level**: Executive level engagement
- **Review Frequency**: Quarterly

**Tactical Vendors**:
- **Definition**: Important for operations but not strategic
- **Characteristics**: Medium spend, medium impact, medium-term relationship
- **Examples**: Hardware vendors, consulting services, specialized tools
- **Management Level**: Manager level engagement
- **Review Frequency**: Semi-annually

**Transactional Vendors**:
- **Definition**: Standard products and services
- **Characteristics**: Low spend, low impact, short-term relationship
- **Examples**: Office supplies, maintenance services, utilities
- **Management Level**: Operational level engagement
- **Review Frequency**: Annually

**Innovation Vendors**:
- **Definition**: Emerging technology and innovation partners
- **Characteristics**: Variable spend, high potential impact, experimental relationship
- **Examples**: Startups, R&D partners, emerging technology vendors
- **Management Level**: Innovation team engagement
- **Review Frequency**: As needed

### Vendor Classification Matrix

| Vendor Type | Spend Level | Business Impact | Relationship Duration | Management Approach |
|-------------|-------------|-----------------|----------------------|-------------------|
| **Strategic** | High | Critical | Long-term | Executive partnership |
| **Tactical** | Medium | Important | Medium-term | Manager relationship |
| **Transactional** | Low | Standard | Short-term | Operational management |
| **Innovation** | Variable | Potential | Experimental | Innovation partnership |

### Vendor Segmentation

**Tier 1 - Strategic Partners**:
- **Count**: 10-15 vendors
- **Spend**: 70-80% of total vendor spend
- **Focus**: Strategic alignment, innovation, long-term value
- **Management**: Dedicated vendor managers, executive engagement

**Tier 2 - Important Vendors**:
- **Count**: 25-35 vendors
- **Spend**: 15-25% of total vendor spend
- **Focus**: Operational excellence, cost optimization
- **Management**: Category managers, regular reviews

**Tier 3 - Standard Vendors**:
- **Count**: 100+ vendors
- **Spend**: 5-10% of total vendor spend
- **Focus**: Cost efficiency, process automation
- **Management**: Automated processes, exception management

## üìã Contract Management

### Contract Types

**Master Service Agreement (MSA)**:
- **Purpose**: Framework agreement for multiple services
- **Duration**: 3-5 years with renewal options
- **Scope**: Broad service categories and capabilities
- **Benefits**: Simplified contracting, volume discounts, relationship building

**Statement of Work (SOW)**:
- **Purpose**: Specific project or service delivery
- **Duration**: Project-based or annual
- **Scope**: Detailed project requirements and deliverables
- **Benefits**: Clear scope definition, milestone-based payments

**Service Level Agreement (SLA)**:
- **Purpose**: Performance standards and metrics
- **Duration**: Aligned with contract term
- **Scope**: Specific performance requirements and penalties
- **Benefits**: Clear performance expectations, accountability

**Software License Agreement**:
- **Purpose**: Software licensing and usage rights
- **Duration**: Annual or perpetual
- **Scope**: Software usage, maintenance, and support
- **Benefits**: Clear usage rights, cost predictability
