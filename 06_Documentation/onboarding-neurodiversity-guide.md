## üéØ Understanding Neurodiversity

### Neurodiversity Framework

#### What is Neurodiversity?
```
# Neurodiversity Definition and Scope

## Core Concept
- [ ] Neurodiversity recognizes neurological differences as natural variations
- [ ] Includes conditions like autism, ADHD, dyslexia, dyspraxia, and others
- [ ] Emphasizes strengths and unique perspectives of neurodivergent individuals
- [ ] Promotes inclusion and accommodation rather than "fixing" differences
- [ ] Recognizes that neurodivergent individuals bring valuable skills and insights
- [ ] Supports diverse thinking and problem-solving approaches

## Common Neurodivergent Conditions
- [ ] Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD): Unique social and sensory experiences
- [ ] Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD): Different attention and focus patterns
- [ ] Dyslexia: Different reading and language processing
- [ ] Dyspraxia: Different motor coordination and planning
- [ ] Dyscalculia: Different mathematical processing
- [ ] Tourette Syndrome: Involuntary movements and vocalizations
```

#### Strengths and Challenges
```
# Neurodivergent Strengths and Challenges

## Common Strengths
- [ ] Pattern recognition and systems thinking
- [ ] Attention to detail and accuracy
- [ ] Creative problem-solving and innovation
- [ ] Hyperfocus and deep concentration
- [ ] Unique perspectives and insights
- [ ] Honesty and direct communication
- [ ] Loyalty and dedication
- [ ] Technical and analytical skills

## Common Challenges
- [ ] Social communication and interaction
- [ ] Sensory processing and sensitivity
- [ ] Executive functioning and organization
- [ ] Time management and planning
- [ ] Change and transition management
- [ ] Information processing and filtering
- [ ] Emotional regulation and stress management
- [ ] Workplace communication and collaboration
```
