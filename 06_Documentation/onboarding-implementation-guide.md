## Common Pitfalls & How to Avoid Them

### ‚ö†Ô∏è Pitfall 1: Information Overload
**Problem**: Dumping too much information in the first week
**Solution**: 
- Spread information across multiple weeks
- Use microlearning and bite-sized content
- Provide reference materials for later review
- Focus on essential information first

### ‚ö†Ô∏è Pitfall 2: Lack of Personal Connection
**Problem**: New hires feel isolated and disconnected
**Solution**:
- Assign dedicated mentors and buddies
- Schedule regular informal check-ins
- Create opportunities for team building
- Use video calls for face-to-face interaction

### ‚ö†Ô∏è Pitfall 3: Unclear Expectations
**Problem**: New hires don't know what success looks like
**Solution**:
- Define clear 30-60-90 day goals
- Provide regular feedback and progress updates
- Use specific, measurable success criteria
- Celebrate achievements and milestones

### ‚ö†Ô∏è Pitfall 4: One-Size-Fits-All Approach
**Problem**: Same onboarding for all roles and experience levels
**Solution**:
- Create role-specific tracks
- Adapt for different experience levels
- Allow for individual learning preferences
- Provide flexible scheduling options

### ‚ö†Ô∏è Pitfall 5: No Follow-up or Support
**Problem**: Onboarding ends after first week
**Solution**:
- Extend support through 90 days
- Provide ongoing mentorship and development
- Regular check-ins and feedback sessions
- Continuous improvement based on feedback
