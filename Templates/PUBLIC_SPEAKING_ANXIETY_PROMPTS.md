**Use When:** You have an upcoming presentation and are feeling anxious

**Template (English):**

```
1. Immediate coping strategies I can use right now
2. Preparation techniques for the next few days
3. On-the-spot techniques for during the presentation
4. A personalized action plan based on my specific triggers
```

## üí° BEST PRACTICES

### 1. Be Specific
- **Good:** "I get sweaty palms and my voice trembles when presenting to executives"
- **Bad:** "I get nervous when speaking"

### 2. Provide Context
- Include audience type, presentation length, topic, and timeframe
- Mention past experiences that are relevant
- Share what you've already tried

### 3. Combine Prompts
- Don't limit yourself to one prompt
- Combine elements from multiple templates
- Create comprehensive requests

### 4. Iterate and Refine
- Use AI responses as starting points
- Ask follow-up questions
- Refine based on what works

### 5. Practice Between Sessions
- Don't just ask for advice‚Äîpractice it
- Return with results and new questions
- Build on previous sessions

### 6. Track Progress
- Note what techniques work for you
- Document your improvement
- Adjust strategies based on results

## ‚ö†Ô∏è IMPORTANT DISCLAIMERS

### When to Seek Professional Help

While these prompts can be very helpful, consider seeking professional help if you experience:

- **Severe panic attacks** before or during presentations
- **Avoidance behavior** that significantly impacts your life or career
- **Physical symptoms** that are severe or persistent
- **Depression or other mental health concerns** related to speaking anxiety
- **Substance use** to cope with speaking anxiety
- **No improvement** after trying multiple self-help approaches
- **Anxiety that extends** beyond public speaking to other areas

### Types of Professional Help Available

- **Therapists/Counselors**: For anxiety disorders, trauma, or underlying mental health issues
- **Speech Coaches**: For skill development and technique refinement
- **Anxiety Specialists**: For specialized anxiety treatment (CBT, exposure therapy)
- **Support Groups**: For peer support and shared experiences
- **Medical Professionals**: For medication evaluation if appropriate

### Limitations of AI Prompts

These prompts are tools for guidance and self-help, but they:
- Cannot replace professional diagnosis or treatment
- May not address underlying psychological issues
- Should be used as part of a comprehensive approach
- Work best when combined with actual practice and, when needed, professional support

```
1. Review content (don't over-rehearse)
2. Prepare materials and backup plans
3. Get good sleep
4. Eat well, stay hydrated
5. Light exercise or movement
6. Review your "why" and value
7. Trust your preparation
```

**Card 2: Day Of (Morning)**
```
1. Light breakfast, avoid caffeine excess
2. Review key points (not full content)
3. Physical warm-up (stretching, movement)
4. Voice warm-up exercises
5. Breathing exercises
6. Positive affirmations
7. Arrive early to venue
```

**Card 3: 1 Hour Before**
```
1. Final content review (key points only)
2. Breathing exercises (5-10 minutes)
3. Power poses or confident postures
4. Visualize success
5. Review your opening
6. Check technology/equipment
7. Connect with friendly faces in audience
```

**Card 4: During Presentation**
```
1. Start with deep breath
2. Make eye contact with friendly faces
3. Remember: audience wants you to succeed
4. Use pauses (they feel longer to you)
5. If you make a mistake, acknowledge and move on
6. Focus on value, not perfection
7. Enjoy sharing your knowledge
```

**Card 5: Post-Presentation**
```
1. Celebrate completing it (regardless of how it went)
2. Reflect on what went well
3. Note what to improve (without self-criticism)
4. Get feedback if appropriate
5. Rest and recover
6. Apply lessons to next presentation
7. Track progress
```
