## ðŸ“Š **A/B Testing Framework**

### **Testing Methodology**

**Sample Size:**
- **Minimum:** 100 recipients per variation
- **Optimal:** 500+ recipients per variation
- **Statistical Significance:** 95% confidence level

**Testing Duration:**
- **Email 1-2:** 3-5 days
- **Email 3-4:** 2-3 days
- **Email 5-6:** 1-2 days

**Metrics to Track:**
- **Open Rate:** Primary metric
- **Click Rate:** Secondary metric
- **Reply Rate:** Engagement metric
- **Demo Booking Rate:** Conversion metric

---

### **Testing Schedule**

**Week 1: Subject Line Tests**
- Test 4 variations of Email 1 subject lines
- Send to 25% of list each
- Analyze results after 3 days

**Week 2: Content Tests**
- Test 4 variations of Email 1 content
- Use winning subject line
- Send to 25% of list each

**Week 3: CTA Tests**
- Test 4 variations of Email 1 CTAs
- Use winning subject line and content
- Send to 25% of list each

**Week 4: Full Sequence Test**
- Test complete sequence with winning elements
- Compare against control group
- Measure end-to-end conversion

## ðŸ’¡ **A/B Testing Best Practices**

### **Testing Guidelines**

**Do's:**
- Test one variable at a time
- Use statistical significance
- Test with sufficient sample size
- Document all test results
- Implement winners quickly
- Test continuously
- Segment your audience
- Test across different time periods

**Don'ts:**
- Don't test too many variables simultaneously
- Don't make decisions without statistical significance
- Don't ignore seasonal variations
- Don't test with too small sample sizes
- Don't let tests run too long
- Don't ignore negative results
- Don't test without clear hypotheses
- Don't forget to document learnings

### **Common Testing Mistakes**

1. **Testing without clear hypotheses**
2. **Insufficient sample sizes**
3. **Testing too many variables**
4. **Ignoring statistical significance**
5. **Not testing long enough**
6. **Not implementing winners**
7. **Testing without segmentation**
8. **Ignoring seasonal factors**
