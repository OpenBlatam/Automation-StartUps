## 1. **Video Concept Ideation**

### **Primary Prompt Template**
```
I am in search of {ADJECTIVE} and {ADJECTIVE} video concepts to advertise my {PRODUCT/SERVICE/IDEA} to {TARGET AUDIENCE}. Could you propose any ideas that will make me unique in comparison to my competitors?
```

### **Usage Instructions**
- Replace `{ADJECTIVE}` with descriptive words (e.g., "innovative", "compelling", "engaging", "emotional", "professional", "cinematic", "authentic", "dynamic")
- Replace `{PRODUCT/SERVICE/IDEA}` with your specific offering
- Replace `{TARGET AUDIENCE}` with your intended viewers (e.g., "millennial entrepreneurs", "B2B decision makers", "tech-savvy professionals", "small business owners", "creative professionals")

### **Examples**

#### **Example 1: SaaS Product**
```
I am in search of innovative and emotionally compelling video concepts to advertise my AI-powered marketing course to millennial entrepreneurs. Could you propose any ideas that will make me unique in comparison to my competitors?
```

#### **Example 2: Service Business**
```
I am in search of professional and authentic video concepts to advertise my consulting services to B2B decision makers in the technology sector. Could you propose any ideas that will make me unique in comparison to my competitors?
```

#### **Example 3: E-commerce Product**
```
I am in search of dynamic and engaging video concepts to advertise my sustainable fashion brand to eco-conscious millennials aged 25-35. Could you propose any ideas that will make me unique in comparison to my competitors?
```

### **Enhanced Variant (More Context)**
```
I am in search of {ADJECTIVE} and {ADJECTIVE} video concepts to advertise my {PRODUCT/SERVICE/IDEA} to {TARGET AUDIENCE}. 

Project Details:
- Video Length: {DURATION}
- Primary Platform: {PLATFORM} (e.g., Instagram, YouTube, TikTok, LinkedIn)
- Brand Tone: {TONE} (e.g., professional, casual, inspirational, humorous)
- Key Message: {CORE MESSAGE}
- Budget Range: {BUDGET RANGE}

Could you propose {NUMBER} unique video concepts that will differentiate me from competitors like {COMPETITOR 1} and {COMPETITOR 2}? For each concept, please include:
1. Core concept and hook
2. Visual style recommendations
3. Key differentiators
4. Estimated production complexity
5. Platform-specific adaptations
```

### **Expected Output**
- **3-5 unique video concept ideas** with detailed descriptions
- **Competitive differentiation strategies** highlighting what makes each concept unique
- **Creative approaches** tailored to your specific audience
- **Visual style recommendations** (color palettes, cinematography styles, animation approaches)
- **Platform-specific adaptations** for different social media channels
- **Production complexity ratings** to help with budget planning
- **Hook suggestions** for maximum engagement

### **Follow-up Prompts**
```
Based on concept {CONCEPT NUMBER}, can you develop:
1. A detailed storyboard outline
2. Specific shot list recommendations
3. Music and sound design suggestions
4. Text overlay and caption strategies
```

## 2. **Budget Allocation & Planning**

### **Primary Prompt Template**
```
{DURATION} --> a video of {DURATION} length
{BUDGET AMOUNT} --> a {BUDGET AMOUNT} budget

```

### **Usage Instructions**
- Replace `{DURATION}` with video length (e.g., "30 seconds", "2 minutes", "5 minutes", "60 seconds")
- Replace `{BUDGET AMOUNT}` with your total budget (e.g., "$5,000", "â‚¬3,500", "Â£2,000", "$10,000")

30 seconds --> a video of 30 seconds length

5 scenes --> 5 different scenes
3 actors --> a cast of 3 actors

How can I efficiently allocate my budget to ensure comprehensive coverage of all production elements while creating a video of 30 seconds length with a $5,000 budget, incorporating 5 different scenes, and involving a cast of 3 actors?

### **Enhanced Variant (Detailed Breakdown Request)**
```
I need a detailed budget allocation for a {DURATION} video with a total budget of {BUDGET AMOUNT}.

Project Requirements:
- {NUMBER} different scenes/locations
- {NUMBER} actors/talent
- {NUMBER} crew members
- Shooting days: {DAYS}
- Post-production timeline: {TIMELINE}

Please provide:
1. Percentage breakdown by phase (Pre-production, Production, Post-production)
2. Line-item budget with specific costs
3. Cost-saving opportunities and alternatives
4. Priority items if budget needs to be reduced
5. Recommended contingency fund (percentage)
6. Regional cost variations (if applicable)
```

### **Expected Output**
- **Detailed budget breakdown by category:**
  - **Pre-production** (15-20%): Scripting, storyboarding, location scouting, casting, permits, insurance
  - **Production** (50-60%): Equipment rental/purchase, crew salaries, talent fees, location fees, catering, transportation
  - **Post-production** (25-30%): Editing, color grading, sound design, music licensing, graphics/animations, revisions
- **Line-item budget** with specific cost ranges
- **Cost-saving recommendations** (rental vs. purchase, DIY alternatives, bulk discounts)
- **Priority allocation suggestions** (what to prioritize if budget is tight)
- **Contingency planning** (recommended 10-15% buffer)
- **Regional cost variations** (if applicable)
- **Budget optimization strategies** for maximum ROI

### **Budget Templates by Scale**

#### **Micro-Budget ($500-$2,000)**
- Focus: DIY equipment, minimal crew, single location
- Allocation: 10% Pre-pro, 40% Production, 50% Post-pro

#### **Small Budget ($2,000-$10,000)**
- Focus: Professional equipment rental, small crew, 2-3 locations
- Allocation: 15% Pre-pro, 55% Production, 30% Post-pro

#### **Medium Budget ($10,000-$50,000)**
- Focus: Full professional crew, multiple locations, premium post-production
- Allocation: 20% Pre-pro, 50% Production, 30% Post-pro

#### **Large Budget ($50,000+)**
- Focus: Premium everything, extensive locations, A-list talent
- Allocation: 20% Pre-pro, 45% Production, 35% Post-pro

## 4. **Equipment & Tools Planning**

### **Primary Prompt Template**
```
I am intending to capture my video in an open environment and need to make certain that I possess all the vital {DESCRIPTIVE WORD} and {DESCRIPTIVE WORD} apparatus. Do you have any suggestions for indispensable tools and equipment that I should incorporate in my production kit?
```

### **Usage Instructions**
- Replace `{DESCRIPTIVE WORD}` with relevant descriptors based on your needs:
  - **Quality level**: "professional", "high-quality", "cinema-grade", "broadcast-quality"
  - **Portability**: "portable", "lightweight", "compact", "mobile"
  - **Durability**: "weather-resistant", "rugged", "durable", "outdoor-ready"
  - **Functionality**: "essential", "specialized", "versatile", "multi-purpose"
- Consider your shooting environment when choosing descriptors

### **Examples**

#### **Example 1: Outdoor/Open Environment**
```

```

## 17. **Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)**

### **Q1: How do I know which prompt to use first?**
**A:** Start with Prompt 1 (Concept Ideation) if you're beginning a new project. If you have a concept but need budget planning, start with Prompt 2. Use the [Quick Decision Guide](#quick-decision-guide) to find the right prompt for your situation.

### **Q2: Can I combine multiple prompts?**
**A:** Yes! In fact, it's recommended. Use Prompt 7.1 (Complete Project Planning) for an all-in-one approach, or combine related prompts (e.g., Budget + Timeline + Risk Management) for comprehensive planning.

### **Q3: How specific should my variables be?**
**A:** The more specific, the better. Instead of "{BUDGET}", use "$5,000". Instead of "{AUDIENCE}", use "millennial entrepreneurs aged 25-35 in the tech industry". Specificity leads to better AI recommendations.

### **Q4: What if the AI gives me recommendations that don't fit my budget?**
**A:** Use Prompt 9.1 (Budget Overruns) or ask follow-up questions like "Can you provide alternatives for a {SMALLER BUDGET} budget?" The AI can adjust recommendations based on constraints.

### **Q5: How do I measure if my video production was successful?**
**A:** Use Section 10 (Metrics & KPIs) to track relevant metrics. Start with Prompt 10.4 (Video Performance Metrics) to identify what to measure based on your objectives.

### **Q6: Can I use these prompts in Spanish?**
**A:** Yes! Section 13 provides all core prompts translated to Spanish. You can also ask the AI to respond in Spanish by adding "Please respond in Spanish" to any English prompt.

### **Q7: What if I'm working with a remote team?**
**A:** Use Prompt 3 (Team Collaboration) with the Enhanced Variant for Remote/Hybrid Teams. Also check Section 16.1 for project management tool integration.

### **Q8: How do I handle equipment failures during production?**
**A:** Use Prompt 9.5 (Equipment Failures) for immediate solutions. Always have backup equipment and use Prompt 4 (Equipment Planning) to plan for contingencies.

### **Q9: What's the difference between the basic and enhanced prompt variants?**
**A:** Basic variants are simpler and faster. Enhanced variants include more context and request more detailed outputs. Use enhanced variants when you need comprehensive planning or have complex requirements.

### **Q10: Can I customize these prompts for my specific industry?**
**A:** Yes! Use Section 8 (Industry-Specific Prompts) for common industries, or use Prompt 15.2 (Template Customization) to create your own custom templates.

## 18. **Glossary of Video Production Terms**

### **A**

**A-Roll**: Primary footage featuring main subjects, dialogue, or key content.

**Aspect Ratio**: The proportional relationship between width and height of a video (e.g., 16:9, 9:16, 1:1).

**Audio Mixing**: The process of combining multiple audio tracks and adjusting levels, EQ, and effects.

**Avid**: Professional video editing software used in broadcast and film production.

---

### **B**

**B-Roll**: Supplemental footage used to support the main narrative, often showing context or visual interest.

**Bitrate**: The amount of data processed per unit of time in video encoding (measured in Mbps).

**Boom Mic**: A microphone mounted on a pole, positioned above or to the side of subjects.

**B-Roll**: Secondary footage used to enhance storytelling and provide visual variety.

---

### **C**

**Call Sheet**: A document distributed to cast and crew with shooting schedule, locations, and contact information.

**Color Grading**: The process of enhancing or altering the color of video footage for aesthetic or creative purposes.

**Color Correction**: Adjusting colors to achieve natural, accurate representation (different from color grading).

**Codec**: A compression/decompression algorithm used to encode and decode video files (e.g., H.264, H.265, ProRes).

**Compositing**: Combining multiple visual elements into a single image or video sequence.

**Cut**: An instantaneous transition from one shot to another.

---

### **D**

**DaVinci Resolve**: Professional color grading and video editing software.

**Dead Cat**: Wind protection for microphones, typically furry coverings that reduce wind noise.

**Depth of Field**: The range of distance that appears acceptably sharp in a shot.

**Dolly**: A wheeled platform used to move the camera smoothly.

**DPI (Dots Per Inch)**: A measure of image resolution, though PPI (Pixels Per Inch) is more accurate for digital.

---

### **E**

**Edit Decision List (EDL)**: A list of edits used to recreate a video edit in different software.

**Export**: The process of rendering a final video file from editing software.

**Exposure**: The amount of light that reaches the camera sensor, controlled by aperture, shutter speed, and ISO.

---

### **F**

**FPS (Frames Per Second)**: The number of individual frames displayed per second in video (e.g., 24, 30, 60).

**Final Cut Pro**: Professional video editing software for macOS.

**Foley**: Sound effects created and recorded to match on-screen actions.

**Frame Rate**: See FPS.

---

### **G**

**Gaffer**: The head of the electrical department, responsible for lighting.

**Gimbal**: A camera stabilization device that uses motors to keep the camera level and smooth.

**Green Screen**: A technique where subjects are filmed against a green background for later compositing.

**Grip**: Crew member responsible for camera support equipment and rigging.

---

### **H**

**H.264**: A widely used video compression codec, good for online distribution.

**H.265 (HEVC)**: A more efficient video codec than H.264, providing better quality at smaller file sizes.

**Hard Cut**: An immediate transition between shots without effects.

**Headroom**: The space between the top of a subject's head and the top of the frame.

---

### **I**

**ISO**: Camera sensitivity setting that controls how the sensor responds to light (higher = more sensitive but more noise).

**Interlaced**: A video scanning method where frames are split into two fields (largely obsolete).

**Interview**: A video format featuring questions and answers, often used in documentaries and corporate videos.

---

### **J**

**J-Cut**: An audio edit where sound from the next scene begins before the visual transition.

**Jump Cut**: An abrupt transition that creates a jarring effect, often used stylistically.

---

### **K**

**Key Light**: The primary light source illuminating the main subject.

**Keyframe**: A point in an animation or effect where a value is set, with software interpolating between keyframes.

---

### **L**

**L-Cut**: An audio edit where sound from the current scene continues into the next scene.

**Lavalier Mic**: A small microphone worn on clothing, typically clipped to a lapel or collar.

**LUT (Look-Up Table)**: A preset color grading file that applies specific color transformations.

**Lower Thirds**: Text graphics positioned in the lower portion of the screen, typically showing names or titles.

---

### **M**

**Motion Graphics**: Animated graphic elements, often including text, shapes, and effects.

**Montage**: A sequence of shots edited together to compress time or convey information quickly.

---

### **N**

**ND Filter (Neutral Density)**: A filter that reduces light entering the lens without affecting color.

**Non-Linear Editing (NLE)**: Digital video editing that allows random access to any frame (e.g., Premiere Pro, Final Cut).

---

### **O**

**Overhead Shot**: A camera angle looking down from above the subject.

**Overlay**: Visual elements (text, graphics, effects) placed on top of video footage.

---

### **P**

**Pan**: Horizontal camera movement, rotating left or right.

**Pixel**: The smallest unit of a digital image or video frame.

**Post-Production**: The phase after filming, including editing, color grading, sound design, and effects.

**Pre-Production**: The planning phase before filming, including scripting, casting, location scouting, and scheduling.

**ProRes**: Apple's professional video codec, known for high quality and editing-friendly compression.

---

### **R**

**Raw Footage**: Unedited video files directly from the camera, before any processing.

**Resolution**: The number of pixels in a video frame (e.g., 1080p, 4K).

**Rough Cut**: An early version of an edit with basic structure but not yet polished.

**Rule of Thirds**: A composition guideline dividing the frame into thirds for better visual balance.

---

### **S**

**Shot List**: A detailed list of all shots planned for a production, including camera angles and movements.

**Slate/Clapperboard**: A device used to mark the beginning of a take with scene and take numbers.

**Sound Design**: The creation and integration of audio elements, including music, effects, and ambience.

**Storyboard**: Visual representations of planned shots, like a comic book version of the video.

---

### **T**

**Tilt**: Vertical camera movement, rotating up or down.

**Timecode**: A system for identifying specific frames in video using hours:minutes:seconds:frames format.

**Tone**: The emotional quality or mood of a video.

**Tracking Shot**: A shot where the camera moves to follow a subject.

**Transcode**: Converting video from one codec or format to another.

---

### **U**

**UGC (User-Generated Content)**: Video content created by users rather than professional producers.

**Underexposed**: An image or video that is too dark due to insufficient light.

---

### **V**

**VFX (Visual Effects)**: Digital effects added in post-production to enhance or create visuals.

**Voice-Over (VO)**: Narration or dialogue recorded separately and added to video.

---

### **W**

**White Balance**: Camera setting that adjusts color temperature to ensure whites appear white under different lighting.

**Wide Shot**: A camera angle showing a broad view of the scene or subject.

**Workflow**: The sequence of steps and processes in video production from start to finish.

---

### **Z**

**Zoom**: Changing the focal length of a lens to make subjects appear closer or farther away.

## 30. **Troubleshooting Quick Reference**

### **30.1 Common Problems & Solutions**

| Problem | Quick Fix | Long-Term Solution |
|---------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Out of Focus** | Check focus before recording | Use focus peaking, external monitor |
| **Poor Audio** | Get closer to mic | Use proper mic placement, monitor levels |
| **Shaky Footage** | Use tripod/gimbal | Invest in stabilization equipment |
| **Overexposed** | Lower ISO, close aperture | Use ND filters, monitor histogram |
| **Underexposed** | Increase ISO, open aperture | Add lighting, use faster lens |
| **Color Inconsistency** | White balance manually | Use color chart, consistent settings |
| **Audio Sync Issues** | Manually sync in post | Use timecode, clapperboard |
| **Storage Full** | Offload footage | Plan storage needs, bring backups |

## 56. **Troubleshooting Guide**

### **56.1 Common Issues & Solutions**

#### **Problem-Solution Matrix**

| Problem | Symptoms | Quick Fix | Long-Term Solution |
|---------|----------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Out of Focus** | Blurry footage | Check focus before recording | Use focus peaking, external monitor |
| **Poor Audio** | Muffled, quiet, or noisy | Get closer to mic, check levels | Proper mic placement, monitor constantly |
| **Shaky Footage** | Unstable video | Use tripod or gimbal | Invest in stabilization equipment |
| **Color Inconsistency** | Shots don't match | White balance manually | Use color chart, consistent settings |
| **Overexposed** | Too bright, blown out | Lower ISO, close aperture | Use ND filters, monitor histogram |
| **Underexposed** | Too dark | Increase ISO, open aperture | Add lighting, use faster lens |
| **Audio Sync Issues** | Audio doesn't match video | Manually sync in post | Use timecode, clapperboard |
| **Storage Full** | Can't record | Offload footage immediately | Plan storage needs, bring backups |
| **Battery Died** | Camera shut off | Use backup battery | Always bring 3x batteries needed |
| **Memory Card Error** | Can't read card | Try different card reader | Format cards before shoot, use quality cards |

### **56.2 Emergency Procedures**

#### **Emergency Response Checklist**
```
EMERGENCY PROCEDURES
====================

EQUIPMENT FAILURE:
1. Stop recording immediately
2. Assess if backup equipment available
3. If yes: Switch to backup, continue
4. If no: Document issue, contact rental house
5. Adjust schedule if needed
6. Communicate with client immediately

TALENT NO-SHOW:
1. Contact talent immediately
2. If delayed: Adjust schedule, shoot other scenes
3. If no-show: Contact backup talent
4. If no backup: Reschedule or recast
5. Document for contract purposes

WEATHER ISSUES:
1. Check weather forecast day before
2. Have indoor backup location ready
3. If outdoor required: Reschedule
4. Communicate changes immediately
5. Adjust timeline accordingly

LOCATION ACCESS DENIED:
1. Contact location contact immediately
2. Negotiate access if possible
3. If denied: Use backup location
4. Document for future reference
5. Adjust schedule and communicate

DATA LOSS:
1. Stop all recording immediately
2. Don't format cards
3. Try data recovery software
4. If critical: Reshoot affected scenes
5. Review backup procedures
```

### **A.3 Metadata Extraction Script**

#### **Python Script: Extract Video Metadata**
```python
#!/usr/bin/env python3
"""
Extract metadata from video files for documentation
"""

import subprocess
import json
from pathlib import Path

def extract_metadata(video_path):
    """Extract video metadata using ffprobe"""
    cmd = [
        'ffprobe',
        '-v', 'quiet',
        '-print_format', 'json',
        '-show_format',
        '-show_streams',
        str(video_path)
    ]
    
    result = subprocess.run(cmd, capture_output=True, text=True)
    data = json.loads(result.stdout)
    
    video_stream = next(s for s in data['streams'] if s['codec_type'] == 'video')
    audio_stream = next(s for s in data['streams'] if s['codec_type'] == 'audio', None)
    
    metadata = {
        'filename': Path(video_path).name,
        'duration': float(data['format']['duration']),
        'size': int(data['format']['size']),
        'video': {
            'codec': video_stream['codec_name'],
            'resolution': f"{video_stream['width']}x{video_stream['height']}",
            'fps': eval(video_stream['r_frame_rate']),
            'bitrate': int(video_stream.get('bit_rate', 0))
        },
        'audio': {
            'codec': audio_stream['codec_name'] if audio_stream else None,
            'sample_rate': audio_stream.get('sample_rate') if audio_stream else None,
            'channels': audio_stream.get('channels') if audio_stream else None
        } if audio_stream else None
    }
    
    return metadata

# Usage
if __name__ == "__main__":
    video = "path/to/video.mp4"
    meta = extract_metadata(video)
    print(json.dumps(meta, indent=2))
```

## APPENDIX G: **Common Scenarios & Solutions**

### **G.1 Scenario-Based Quick Solutions**

#### **Quick Solution Matrix**
```
COMMON SCENARIOS & QUICK SOLUTIONS
===================================

SCENARIO: Client wants changes after final approval
SOLUTION:
1. Review contract for revision limits
2. Assess if changes are within scope
3. If out of scope, quote additional cost
4. If within scope, make changes
5. Update contract for future clarity

SCENARIO: Equipment fails during shoot
SOLUTION:
1. Stop shooting immediately
2. Check backup equipment availability
3. If available, switch and continue
4. If not, contact rental house
5. Adjust schedule if needed
6. Document for insurance

SCENARIO: Talent doesn't show up
SOLUTION:
1. Contact talent immediately
2. If delayed, shoot other scenes
3. If no-show, contact backup talent
4. If no backup, reschedule
5. Document for contract purposes

SCENARIO: Location access denied
SOLUTION:
1. Contact location contact
2. Negotiate access if possible
3. If denied, use backup location
4. Adjust schedule
5. Document for future reference

SCENARIO: Budget running over
SOLUTION:
1. Review current spending
2. Identify areas to cut
3. Communicate with client
4. Propose solutions
5. Get approval for changes
6. Document everything
```

## APPENDIX H: **Glossary of Terms**

### **H.1 Video Production Terminology**

#### **Complete Terminology Guide**
```
VIDEO PRODUCTION GLOSSARY
=========================

A-ROLL: Primary footage, usually interviews or main action
B-ROLL: Secondary footage used to support A-roll
COLOR GRADING: Post-production process of adjusting colors
COLOR CORRECTION: Fixing color issues in post-production
CRF (Constant Rate Factor): Video quality setting
DAILY: Daily production report
DP (Director of Photography): Person responsible for camera work
FPS (Frames Per Second): Frame rate of video
LUFS (Loudness Units Full Scale): Audio loudness measurement
ND FILTER: Neutral density filter for exposure control
PROXY: Lower resolution file used for editing
ROUGH CUT: First edit version
STRINGOUT: Assembly of best takes
TIMECODE: Time reference in video
WHITE BALANCE: Color temperature adjustment
```
