  - Mobile: 62%
  - Desktop: 32%

**Expected ChatGPT Output:**
1. **Key Insights**:
   - High new visitor percentage (70%) indicates strong acquisition but low retention

   - Organic traffic is primary source (good SEO foundation)
   - Blog posts have lower bounce rate than homepage (content quality)
   - Mobile bounce likely higher than desktop (needs investigation)

   - Social media amplification: Increase posting frequency by 40%
   - Paid advertising: Expand retargeting campaigns
   - Influencer partnerships: 5 micro-influencer collaborations/month
   - Guest posting: 3 high-authority site contributions/month

4. **Content Optimization Strategies**:
   - Refresh top 10 underperforming blog posts
   - Add internal linking to increase pages per session
   - Create content clusters around top-performing topics
   - Implement content upgrade offers (lead magnets)

## Question 2: Essential KPIs for Content Marketing Strategy

### **Critical Performance Indicators to Track**

#### **Primary KPIs (Must-Track)**

1. **Content Performance KPIs**

   **Content Views**
   - **Formula**: `Total Views = Sum of all content page views`
   - **Benchmark by Industry**:
     - SaaS: 50K-200K/month
     - E-commerce: 100K-500K/month
     - B2B Services: 20K-100K/month
     - Media/Content: 500K-2M/month
   - **Tracking**: Google Analytics, Content Management System

   **Engagement Rate**
   - **Formula**: `Engagement Rate = (Likes + Comments + Shares + Saves) / Impressions √ó 100`
   - **Benchmark by Platform**:
     - Facebook: 3-6% (good), 6-9% (excellent)
     - Instagram: 4-7% (good), 7-12% (excellent)
     - LinkedIn: 2-4% (good), 4-8% (excellent)
     - Twitter: 1-3% (good), 3-6% (excellent)
   - **Tracking**: Native platform analytics, social media management tools

   **Time on Page**
   - **Formula**: `Average Time on Page = Total Time on Page / Total Page Views`
   - **Benchmark by Content Type**:
     - Blog posts: 2-4 minutes (good), 4-6 minutes (excellent)
     - Product pages: 1-2 minutes (good), 2-3 minutes (excellent)
     - Landing pages: 30-60 seconds (good), 60-90 seconds (excellent)
   - **Tracking**: Google Analytics, heat mapping tools

   **Scroll Depth**
   - **Formula**: `Scroll Depth = (Users who scrolled to X% / Total Users) √ó 100`
   - **Benchmark**: 
     - 25% scroll: 80-90% of users
     - 50% scroll: 60-70% of users
     - 75% scroll: 40-50% of users
     - 100% scroll: 20-30% of users
   - **Tracking**: Google Analytics events, scroll tracking tools

   **Bounce Rate**
   - **Formula**: `Bounce Rate = (Single-page sessions / Total sessions) √ó 100`
   - **Benchmark by Industry**:
     - SaaS: 40-50% (good), <40% (excellent)
     - E-commerce: 45-55% (good), <45% (excellent)
     - Blogs: 50-70% (good), <50% (excellent)
     - Landing pages: 60-80% (acceptable), <60% (excellent)
   - **Tracking**: Google Analytics

   **Pages per Session**
   - **Formula**: `Pages per Session = Total Page Views / Total Sessions`
   - **Benchmark**: 2-3 pages (good), 3-5 pages (excellent)
   - **Tracking**: Google Analytics

2. **Traffic KPIs**

   **Organic Traffic Growth Rate**
   - **Formula**: `Growth Rate = ((Current Month - Previous Month) / Previous Month) √ó 100`
   - **Benchmark**: 10-20% MoM (good), 20%+ MoM (excellent)
   - **Tracking**: Google Analytics, Google Search Console

   **Traffic Source Distribution**
   - **Formula**: `Source % = (Traffic from Source / Total Traffic) √ó 100`
   - **Ideal Distribution**:
     - Organic: 40-50%
     - Direct: 15-25%
     - Social: 15-20%
     - Paid: 10-15%
     - Referral: 5-10%
   - **Tracking**: Google Analytics UTM parameters

3. **Engagement KPIs**

   **Social Shares**
   - **Formula**: `Total Shares = Sum of shares across all platforms`
   - **Benchmark**: 100-500 shares/month (good), 500+ shares/month (excellent)
   - **Tracking**: Social media analytics, share tracking tools

   **Email Signup Rate**
   - **Formula**: `Signup Rate = (Email Signups / Total Visitors) √ó 100`
   - **Benchmark**: 1-2% (good), 2-5% (excellent)
   - **Tracking**: Email marketing platforms, form analytics

   **Video Completion Rate**
   - **Formula**: `Completion Rate = (Video Completions / Video Starts) √ó 100`
   - **Benchmark**: 40-60% (good), 60%+ (excellent)
   - **Tracking**: YouTube Analytics, video platform analytics

4. **Conversion KPIs**

   **Lead Generation Rate**
   - **Formula**: `Lead Rate = (Leads Generated / Content Views) √ó 100`
   - **Benchmark by Content Type**:
     - Blog posts: 0.5-1% (good), 1-2% (excellent)
     - Landing pages: 5-10% (good), 10-20% (excellent)
     - Webinars: 20-30% (good), 30%+ (excellent)
   - **Tracking**: CRM systems, form analytics

   **Cost per Lead (CPL)**
   - **Formula**: `CPL = Total Marketing Cost / Total Leads Generated`
   - **Benchmark by Industry**:
     - SaaS: $50-150 (good), <$50 (excellent)
     - E-commerce: $20-50 (good), <$20 (excellent)
     - B2B Services: $100-300 (good), <$100 (excellent)
   - **Tracking**: Marketing automation platforms, CRM

   **Lead Quality Score**
   - **Formula**: `Quality Score = (Qualified Leads / Total Leads) √ó 100`
   - **Benchmark**: 60-70% (good), 70%+ (excellent)
   - **Tracking**: CRM lead scoring, sales team feedback

5. **Revenue KPIs**

   **Content ROI**
   - **Formula**: `Content ROI = ((Revenue from Content - Content Cost) / Content Cost) √ó 100`
   - **Benchmark**: 300-500% (good), 500%+ (excellent)
   - **Tracking**: Revenue attribution, marketing analytics

   **Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)**
   - **Formula**: `CAC = Total Marketing & Sales Cost / New Customers Acquired`
   - **Benchmark by Industry**:
     - SaaS: $200-500 (good), <$200 (excellent)
     - E-commerce: $50-150 (good), <$50 (excellent)
   - **Tracking**: Marketing analytics, CRM

   **Lifetime Value (LTV)**
   - **Formula**: `LTV = Average Order Value √ó Purchase Frequency √ó Customer Lifespan`
   - **Benchmark**: LTV should be 3-5x CAC
   - **Tracking**: Customer analytics, CRM

   **LTV/CAC Ratio**
   - **Formula**: `LTV/CAC Ratio = Lifetime Value / Customer Acquisition Cost`
   - **Benchmark**: 3:1 (minimum), 5:1+ (excellent)
   - **Tracking**: Financial analytics, CRM

# Google Analytics 4 API - Automated Data Export
# Install: pip install google-analytics-data

from google.analytics.data_v1beta import BetaAnalyticsDataClient
from google.analytics.data_v1beta.types import (
    DateRange,
    Dimension,
    Metric,
    RunReportRequest,
)
import pandas as pd
from datetime import datetime, timedelta

def export_ga4_data(property_id, start_date, end_date):
    """
    Export Google Analytics 4 data for ChatGPT analysis
    """
    client = BetaAnalyticsDataClient()
    
    request = RunReportRequest(
        property=f"properties/{property_id}",
        date_ranges=[DateRange(start_date=start_date, end_date=end_date)],
        dimensions=[
            Dimension(name="date"),
            Dimension(name="source"),
            Dimension(name="medium"),
            Dimension(name="pagePath"),
        ],
        metrics=[
            Metric(name="sessions"),
            Metric(name="users"),
            Metric(name="engagementRate"),
            Metric(name="bounceRate"),
            Metric(name="conversions"),
        ],
    )
    
    response = client.run_report(request)
    
    # Convert to DataFrame
    rows = []
    for row in response.rows:
        rows.append({
            'date': row.dimension_values[0].value,
            'source': row.dimension_values[1].value,
            'medium': row.dimension_values[2].value,
            'page': row.dimension_values[3].value,
            'sessions': row.metric_values[0].value,
            'users': row.metric_values[1].value,
            'engagement_rate': row.metric_values[2].value,
            'bounce_rate': row.metric_values[3].value,
            'conversions': row.metric_values[4].value,
        })
    
    df = pd.DataFrame(rows)
    
    # Export to CSV for ChatGPT
    filename = f"ga4_data_{start_date}_to_{end_date}.csv"
    df.to_csv(filename, index=False)
    
    return filename, df

# Usage
property_id = "YOUR_PROPERTY_ID"

## üìà Advanced Metrics & Formulas

### **Content Velocity Metrics**

#### **Content Production Velocity**
```
Formula: Content Velocity = Total Content Pieces / Time Period

Example:
- 30 blog posts in 30 days = 1 post/day
- Velocity score: 1.0

Benchmark:
- Low: < 0.5 posts/day
- Medium: 0.5-1.0 posts/day
- High: 1.0-2.0 posts/day
- Very High: > 2.0 posts/day
```

#### **Content Performance Velocity**
```
Formula: Performance Velocity = (Current Performance - Previous Performance) / Time Period

Example:
- Traffic growth: 10,000 to 15,000 in 30 days
- Velocity: (15,000 - 10,000) / 30 = 166.67 visitors/day

Benchmark:
- Low: < 50/day
- Medium: 50-200/day
- High: 200-500/day
- Very High: > 500/day
```

### **Content Efficiency Metrics**

#### **Content Efficiency Ratio**
```
Formula: Efficiency = (Total Revenue / Total Content Pieces) / (Total Cost / Total Content Pieces)

Example:
- Revenue: $100,000
- Content pieces: 50
- Cost: $50,000
- Efficiency = ($100,000/50) / ($50,000/50) = $2,000 / $1,000 = 2.0

Interpretation:
- < 1.0: Inefficient
- 1.0-2.0: Moderate efficiency
- 2.0-3.0: Good efficiency
- > 3.0: High efficiency
```

#### **Content Quality Score**
```
Formula: Quality Score = (Engagement Rate √ó 0.4) + (Time on Page √ó 0.3) + (Conversion Rate √ó 0.3)

Components:
- Engagement Rate: (Likes + Comments + Shares) / Impressions
- Time on Page: Minutes spent reading
- Conversion Rate: Conversions / Views

Scoring:
- 0-30: Low quality
- 30-60: Medium quality
- 60-80: High quality
- 80-100: Excellent quality
```
