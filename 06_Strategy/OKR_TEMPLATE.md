## üéØ **OKR BEST PRACTICES**

### **Objective Writing:**
‚úÖ DO:
- Be inspiring and aspirational
- Make it clear and concise
- Focus on outcomes, not activities
- Keep time-bound (quarterly)

‚ùå DON'T:
- Use metrics in objectives
- Make it too easy or too hard
- Be vague or unclear
- Have too many objectives

### **Key Results Writing:**
‚úÖ DO:
- Make it specific and measurable
- Use metrics not opinions
- Set stretch targets (70-100% achievement)
- Track weekly

‚ùå DON'T:
- Make them binary (yes/no)
- Use vague words ("better", "improve")
- Set targets too low or impossible
- Have key results that contradict each other

### **Number of OKRs:**
- **Company level**: 3-5 objectives
- **Team level**: 2-3 objectives per team
- **Individual level**: 1-2 objectives
- **Total key results**: 3-5 per objective

## üéØ **GETTING STARTED**

### **Step 1: Define 3-5 Objectives** (30 min)
- What do we want to achieve this quarter?
- Why does each matter?
- Are they aligned with strategy?

### **Step 2: Create Key Results** (30 min)
- How will we measure success?
- Are they specific and measurable?
- Do they inspire action?

### **Step 3: Set Up Tracking** (15 min)
- Choose tracking system
- Input targets
- Set baseline measurements

### **Step 4: Launch** (15 min)
- Communicate OKRs
- Assign owners
- Start executing

### **Step 5: Review Regularly**
- Weekly progress checks
- Monthly reviews
- Quarterly evaluation
