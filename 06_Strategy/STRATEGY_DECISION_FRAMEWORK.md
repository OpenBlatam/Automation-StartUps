## ðŸŽ¯ **QUICK DECISION PATTERNS**

### **The 10-Minute Decision**
Use when decision is low-stakes:

1. **Define** (2 min): What's the decision?
2. **Criteria** (1 min): What matters most?
3. **Options** (2 min): What are the choices?
4. **Decide** (1 min): Pick the best option
5. **Action** (4 min): Start implementing

### **The 1-Hour Decision**
Use when decision is medium-stakes:

1. **Define** (10 min): Document the decision
2. **Gather** (20 min): Collect key information
3. **Evaluate** (20 min): Assess options
4. **Decide** (10 min): Make the call

### **The Strategic Decision (4 hours)**
Use for high-stakes decisions:

1. **Define** (30 min): Comprehensive documentation
2. **Gather** (90 min): Deep research
3. **Evaluate** (90 min): Thorough analysis
4. **Impact** (30 min): Risk assessment
5. **Decide** (30 min): Final decision and plan

## ðŸŽ¯ **SPECIALIZED DECISION FRAMEWORKS**

### **GO/NO-GO FRAMEWORK**
For project/investment decisions:

**GO Criteria (All must be Yes):**
- [ ] Strategic alignment: Clear fit with strategy
- [ ] Resource availability: Have required resources
- [ ] Success probability: >70% chance of success
- [ ] Value creation: Clear ROI or strategic value
- [ ] Risk tolerance: Acceptable risk level

**Decision**: [Go / No-Go]

### **BUILD/BUY/PARTNER FRAMEWORK**
For capability decisions:

**Consider Building If:**
- Strategic advantage
- Core competency
- Available talent
- Time allowed

**Consider Buying If:**
- Market solution exists
- Faster to market
- Not core competency
- ROI positive

**Consider Partnering If:**
- Complementary strengths
- Faster execution
- Shared risk
- Mutual benefit

### **PIVOT/HOLD/DOUBLE-DOWN**
For performance decisions:

**Pivot If:**
- Not meeting goals for 3+ months
- Market changed significantly
- Strategy proving ineffective

**Hold If:**
- Meeting goals
- Progress trending positive
- Too early to tell

**Double-Down If:**
- Exceeding goals
- High growth potential
- Clear competitive advantage

## ðŸŽ¯ **BEST PRACTICES**

### **Decision-Making Principles**
1. **Bias Awareness**: Know your biases
2. **Data-Driven**: Use data, not just opinions
3. **Inclusive**: Get diverse perspectives
4. **Timely**: Don't over-analyze
5. **Reversible**: Consider undo costs
6. **Measurable**: Define success criteria
7. **Communication**: Share decisions clearly

### **Common Decision Traps**
1. **Analysis Paralysis**: Gathering too much info
2. **Confirmation Bias**: Only seeing confirming data
3. **Anchoring**: Stuck on first piece of info
4. **Sunk Cost Fallacy**: Continuing bad investments
5. **Groupthink**: Everyone agrees too easily
6. **Omission Bias**: Preferring inaction to wrong action
7. **Framing Effect**: How question is asked influences

### **How to Avoid Traps**
- Use this framework systematically
- Get outside perspectives
- Challenge assumptions
- Set decision deadlines
- Review past decisions
- Measure outcomes
- Learn and improve
