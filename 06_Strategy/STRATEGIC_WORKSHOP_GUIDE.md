## ðŸŽ¯ **FACILITATION TECHNIQUES**

### **Opening Techniques**

#### **Ice Breaker: Strategic Speed Dating**
**Time**: 15 minutes
**Purpose**: Get participants talking about strategy

**Instructions**:
1. Pair participants
2. Each person shares: "One strategic challenge we face"
3. Partner listens and asks one clarifying question
4. Switch roles
5. Find new partner and repeat

#### **Vision Exercise: Future Headlines**
**Time**: 20 minutes
**Purpose**: Create inspiring vision

**Instructions**:
1. Ask: "What headlines would you like to see about your company in 5 years?"
2. Participants write headlines on post-its
3. Share and group similar headlines
4. Discuss themes and aspirations

### **Analysis Techniques**

#### **SWOT Analysis: Silent Brainstorming**
**Time**: 30 minutes
**Purpose**: Generate comprehensive SWOT

**Instructions**:
1. Create 4 flip charts (S, W, O, T)
2. Silent brainstorming for 10 minutes
3. Each person adds 3-5 items per category
4. Group similar items
5. Prioritize top 5 in each category

#### **Competitive Analysis: Battle Cards**
**Time**: 45 minutes
**Purpose**: Understand competitive landscape

**Instructions**:
1. Create competitor cards
2. For each competitor, identify:
   - Strengths
   - Weaknesses
   - Strategy
   - Threats to us
3. Share insights
4. Identify opportunities

### **Planning Techniques**

#### **Strategic Objectives: Dot Voting**
**Time**: 30 minutes
**Purpose**: Prioritize objectives

**Instructions**:
1. List all potential objectives
2. Each person gets 5 dots
3. Vote on most important objectives
4. Select top 3-5 objectives
5. Refine wording

#### **Initiative Planning: Impact vs Effort Matrix**
**Time**: 45 minutes
**Purpose**: Prioritize initiatives

**Instructions**:
1. Plot initiatives on 2x2 matrix
2. X-axis: Effort (Low to High)
3. Y-axis: Impact (Low to High)
4. Focus on High Impact, Low Effort
5. Plan High Impact, High Effort carefully

## ðŸ“Š **WORKSHOP ACTIVITIES**

### **Activity 1: Strategic Position Assessment**

**Objective**: Assess current strategic position
**Time**: 45 minutes
**Materials**: SWOT template, markers

**Process**:
1. **Strengths** (10 min): What do we do exceptionally well?
2. **Weaknesses** (10 min): Where do we struggle?
3. **Opportunities** (10 min): What trends favor us?
4. **Threats** (10 min): What threatens our success?
5. **Discussion** (5 min): Key insights and implications

**Output**: Completed SWOT analysis

---

### **Activity 2: Vision & Mission Development**

**Objective**: Create compelling vision and mission
**Time**: 60 minutes
**Materials**: Vision template, markers

**Process**:
1. **Vision Exercise** (20 min): Future headlines activity
2. **Mission Discussion** (20 min): Why do we exist?
3. **Values Identification** (20 min): What matters most?

**Output**: Draft vision, mission, and values

---

### **Activity 3: Strategic Objectives Setting**

**Objective**: Define strategic objectives
**Time**: 60 minutes
**Materials**: OKR template, markers

**Process**:
1. **Objective Brainstorming** (20 min): What do we want to achieve?
2. **Priority Setting** (20 min): Dot voting exercise
3. **OKR Development** (20 min): Key results for each objective

**Output**: 3-5 strategic objectives with key results

---

### **Activity 4: Initiative Planning**

**Objective**: Plan strategic initiatives
**Time**: 90 minutes
**Materials**: Initiative template, markers

**Process**:
1. **Initiative Identification** (30 min): What initiatives support objectives?
2. **Impact Assessment** (30 min): Impact vs effort matrix
3. **Resource Planning** (30 min): Budget, team, timeline

**Output**: Prioritized initiatives with resource plans

- **Prepare thoroughly**: Know your material

- **Assign action items**: Clear ownership and deadlines
- **Schedule reviews**: Regular check-ins
- **Celebrate progress**: Recognize achievements

## ðŸŽ¯ **COMMON WORKSHOP CHALLENGES**

### **Challenge 1: Dominant Participants**
**Problem**: One person dominates discussion
**Solution**: 
- Use round-robin techniques
- Ask specific people for input
- Use silent brainstorming
- Set speaking time limits

### **Challenge 2: Quiet Participants**
**Problem**: Some people don't contribute
**Solution**:
- Use pair activities first
- Ask direct questions
- Use written exercises
- Create safe environment

### **Challenge 3: Off-Topic Discussions**
**Problem**: Conversations go off track
**Solution**:
- Use parking lot for off-topic items
- Redirect to agenda
- Use time limits
- Stay focused on objectives

### **Challenge 4: Conflict**
**Problem**: Disagreements become personal
**Solution**:
- Focus on issues, not people
- Use data to support points
- Find common ground
- Take breaks if needed
