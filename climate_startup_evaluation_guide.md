# Climate Startup Evaluation Guide
## Sector-Specific Criteria for Climate Investments

### Key Climate Evaluation Dimensions

#### 1. Climate Impact (Critical)
**Carbon Reduction Potential**
- Quantified CO2 reduction per unit
- Scalability of climate impact
- Measurement and verification methods
- Additionality vs. existing solutions

**Technology Readiness Level (TRL)**
- TRL 1-3: Basic research
- TRL 4-6: Technology development
- TRL 7-9: System demonstration to deployment
- Clear path to commercialization

**Regulatory Tailwinds**
- Government incentives and subsidies
- Carbon pricing mechanisms
- Regulatory mandates
- Policy support and stability

#### 2. Market Opportunity (Critical)
**Market Size & Growth**
- Total addressable market for climate solutions
- Market growth rate and drivers
- Customer willingness to pay
- Market timing and readiness

**Customer Segments**
- Enterprise vs. consumer focus
- B2B vs. B2C business models
- Customer concentration risk
- Sales cycle characteristics

#### 3. Technology & Innovation (Important)
**Technical Differentiation**
- Novel approaches to climate problems
- Patent protection and IP
- Technical barriers to entry
- R&D investment and capabilities

**Scalability**
- Manufacturing scalability
- Supply chain considerations
- Capital intensity requirements
- Operational complexity

#### 4. Business Model (Important)
**Revenue Model**
- Product vs. service revenue
- Recurring vs. one-time sales
- Pricing strategy and elasticity
- Customer retention and expansion

**Unit Economics**
- Cost structure and margins
- Customer acquisition costs
- Lifetime value calculations
- Path to profitability

### Climate-Specific Red Flags

#### Technical Red Flags
- Unproven technology at early TRL
- High capital requirements
- Complex supply chains
- Regulatory uncertainty
- Long development timelines

#### Market Red Flags
- Limited customer willingness to pay
- Long sales cycles
- High customer acquisition costs
- Market not ready for adoption
- Competitive pressure from incumbents

#### Business Red Flags
- Poor unit economics
- High burn rate
- Limited scalability
- Customer concentration risk
- Dependency on government incentives

### Climate Sector Benchmarks

#### Early Stage (Pre-Seed/Seed)
- **Team**: 2-5 people, strong technical founders
- **Traction**: 0-5 customers, early pilots
- **Revenue**: $0-25K MRR
- **Valuation**: $5-15M pre-money

#### Growth Stage (Series A)
- **Team**: 10-25 people, complete founding team
- **Traction**: 25-100 customers, product-market fit
- **Revenue**: $50K-500K MRR
- **Valuation**: $15-75M pre-money

#### Scale Stage (Series B)
- **Team**: 25-100 people, experienced management
- **Traction**: 100+ customers, strong growth
- **Revenue**: $500K+ MRR
- **Valuation**: $75M+ pre-money

### Key Questions for Climate Startups

#### Technical Questions
1. What's your technology readiness level?
2. How do you measure climate impact?
3. What are the technical barriers to entry?
4. How do you ensure quality and reliability?
5. What's your R&D roadmap?

#### Business Questions
1. What's the ROI for your customers?
2. How do you price your climate solutions?
3. What are your customer acquisition costs?
4. How do you handle enterprise sales cycles?
5. What's your competitive differentiation?

#### Market Questions
1. How large is your addressable market?
2. What's the adoption timeline for your solution?
3. How do you handle regulatory compliance?
4. What partnerships are critical to your success?
5. How do you scale your go-to-market strategy?
